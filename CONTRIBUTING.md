# Contributing to the Regulated AI Deployment Checklist

Thank you for considering a contribution. The checklist is meant to be a living document that reflects real operational practice across regulated AI deployments. Improvements from practitioners who have run through it on the ground are the most valuable.

## What kinds of contribution help

- **New items.** A control or check that should be on the list but is not yet. Include a sentence on why a regulated-AI deployment needs it.
- **Reworded items.** An item whose current wording is ambiguous, jargon-heavy, or technically inaccurate.
- **New sections.** A whole category that is missing (suggested in an issue first).
- **Sector-specific forks.** Healthcare-specific, financial-services-specific, or public-sector-specific variants. We will link to them from the README as community forks rather than merge them into the main list.
- **Translations.** The list is licensed CC BY 4.0; translations are welcome as forks.

## What does not belong

- Generic AI ethics commentary or philosophy.
- Items that only apply to a single vendor or a single product.
- Items where the answer is "it depends" without specific operational guidance.
- Items that read as marketing for a specific tool.

## How to propose a change

1. Open an issue first if the change is more than a sentence or two of wording. We will discuss the scope before you write the PR.
2. For a single new item: open the PR directly. Add the item to the relevant Phase section. Use the format `- [ ] **Bold lede.** One or two sentences on the operational meaning.`
3. Tick the box on items you have personally implemented if you are commenting on a real deployment. Tick nothing if this is just commentary.

## Style

- British English in the item copy.
- No em-dashes used as sentence punctuation. Compound hyphens are fine.
- No emojis.
- Plain operational language. Avoid banned vocabulary (delve, leverage, harness, robust, seamless, holistic, transformative).

## Sector-specific forks

If you maintain a sector-specific fork (healthcare, financial services, public sector, manufacturing, education), open an issue here and we will link from the main README. Keep your fork under your own organisation; we will not merge sector-specific variants into the main list because they grow it unmanageably.

## Questions

Open a GitHub Discussion or a low-priority issue tagged `question`.
